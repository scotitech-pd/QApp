# Q-App Customer Pain Journey

## Product Feeling

Q-App should feel like relief from uncertain waiting.

The customer is not opening the app because they want software. They are opening it because they want to know:

- Is it worth going now?
- How long will I really wait?
- Can I hold my place without standing there?
- Will I know when to leave or confirm?

## Simple Customer Journey

### 1. Open Q-App

Customer sees nearby live queues immediately.

The first screen must answer:

- shop name
- distance if available
- live wait time
- how many people are waiting
- whether the queue is open

### 2. Choose a Shop

Customer chooses based on time, not guesswork.

Do not show complex service selection, staff selection, or booking rules in the MVP queue journey.

### 3. Join Queue

Customer enters only:

- name
- mobile number

Then Q-App sends a verification code and creates the queue entry after verification.

No account creation. No last initial. No provider choice.

### 4. Wait Somewhere Better

Customer status page shows:

- current place
- estimated wait
- queue size
- clear next action
- live state when called, seated/in service, or completed
- per-visit saved waiting time where timing data is available

Language should be human, not operational. Use "Your place is held" instead of internal queue terms.

### 5. Confirm Arrival

When the shop needs the customer, Q-App asks:

- Are you coming?
- Yes, I am coming
- No, release my place

If the customer does not respond, Q-App can release the place and promote the next customer.

### 6. Finish and Feedback

When the owner starts service, the customer page should show that the customer is now being served.

When the owner completes service, the customer page should show:

- thank-you message
- approximate waiting time saved for that visit
- optional feedback form

Lifetime saved-time stats should wait for customer accounts. Do not fake cross-visit totals without login.

## MVP UX Rules

- Show wait time before asking for customer details.
- Keep the customer path account-free.
- Keep queue join to name and phone only.
- Use shop-level queues only.
- Make every screen answer: "What should I do now?"
- Avoid internal words like portal, ops, visit status, and lifecycle on customer screens.

## Future Secret Sauce

Smart arrival should later calculate:

- queue progress
- customer location
- travel time
- when to leave

That future capability should feel like: "Leave in 7 minutes", not like a map feature bolted onto a queue.
